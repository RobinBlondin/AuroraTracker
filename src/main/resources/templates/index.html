How To Subscribe To Aurora Alerts

For the best experience, open this page on your mobile device and add it to your home screen. Doing so allows Aurora Tracker to send you push notifications whenever the northern lights may be visible near your location.

To subscribe, simply press the green button and allow both location access and notifications when prompted. Your current coordinates will be securely stored in our database to calculate the likelihood of aurora activity in your area. If conditions indicate visibility, you’ll receive a single notification for that event, but auroras often remain visible for several hours afterward.

Pressing the green button again will update your saved location. This is useful if you’re traveling and want to receive alerts based on your temporary position.

Aurora Tracker only stores your latitude and longitude. We do not collect or use any other personal information, your data is used exclusively to deliver aurora alerts relevant to your location.

If you wish to unsubscribe, simply press the red button. Your location data will be permanently removed from our system immediately.

How Aurora Tracker Works

Aurora Tracker keeps an eye on geomagnetic activity around the world and alerts you when the Northern Lights might be visible from your location.

The system gathers live data from space weather services and calculates how far the auroral oval extends based on the current Kp index, a global measure of geomagnetic disturbance.

Your position is compared to the edge of this auroral zone using precise geographic formulas (yes, real math stolen from very skilled mathematicians!). If conditions look promising, Aurora Tracker sends you a notification so you don’t miss the show in the sky.
